🔎 What is Sorting?

Sorting is the process of arranging data in a specific order, usually ascending (smallest to largest) or descending (largest to smallest). It makes data easier to search, analyze, and visualize.

✨ Why is Sorting Important?

  • Helps in faster searching (example: Binary Search requires sorted data).
  • Makes data organization simple and clear.
  • Improves efficiency of other algorithms like searching, graph, and database operations.
  • Used in real-time systems like airline ticketing, e-commerce, and banking.

📂 Types of Sorting Techniques

  • Bubble Sort – Simple method, repeatedly swaps adjacent elements if they are in wrong order.
  • Selection Sort – Finds the minimum element and places it at the beginning.
  • Insertion Sort – Builds the final sorted array one item at a time.

🌍 Real-Life Applications of Sorting

  • E-commerce: Sorting products by price, popularity, or rating.
  • Search Engines: Ranking search results based on relevance.
  • Banking: Sorting customer records or transactions by date or amount.
  • Airline/Train Systems: Arranging passenger lists or schedules.
  • Data Science: Preprocessing datasets before analysis and visualization.
